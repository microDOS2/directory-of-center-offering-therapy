Pricing for psilocybin therapy varies by state and facility type. Insurance typically does not cover psilocybin therapy (though preparation/integration may be covered).

Financial Assistance

  • • Forward Fund: Weighted lottery system (Althea & Tricycle Day partnership)
  • • Veterans Discounts: Many centers offer reduced rates (e.g., Satya $850 vs $800)
  • • Sliding Scale: Omnia Group, River Soul Healing offer income-based pricing
  • • Insurance: Some centers accept insurance for prep/integration (not medicine)

Cost-Saving Tips

  • • Group sessions: $2,850 vs $3,400 individual (significant savings)
  • • Oregon vs CO: Oregon starts at $800 (Satya) vs CO average $2,800-$3,500
  • • Microdosing: $150-$300 sessions available at Vivid Minds (CO)
  • • Nonprofit centers: EPIC Healing offers grants for underserved communities

Legal & Regulatory Framework

Complete guide to psilocybin laws in Colorado and Oregon. Federal Status: Schedule I Controlled Substance

Proposition 122

Natural Medicine Health Act passed November 2022.

  • Decriminalized personal use (21+)
  • Created regulated healing center model
  • Applications opened Dec 31, 2024

Regulatory Bodies

Department of Revenue
Natural Medicine Division - Business licensing (healing centers, cultivators, manufacturers)
DORA
Department of Regulatory Agencies - Facilitator & clinical facilitator licensing

Permitted Substances

Now Available: Psilocybin, Psilocin
After June 2026: DMT, Ibogaine, Mescaline*
*Excluding peyote

Prohibited in Colorado

  • • Retail sales to public (administration only)
  • • Take-home psilocybin products
  • • Synthetic psilocybin (natural only)
  • • Medical claims in advertising
  • • Operation without state license

Requirements

  • • Age 21+ for all participants
  • • Licensed facilitator present entire session
  • • Preparation session before administration
  • • On-site consumption only
  • • Third-party testing of all products

Measure 109

Oregon Psilocybin Services Act passed November 2020.

  • First state to legalize (nation's first)
  • Applications opened Jan 2, 2023
  • First center opened June 2023 (EPIC)

Regulatory Body

Oregon Health Authority
Psilocybin Services Section
Licenses: Service Centers, Manufacturers, Testing Labs, Facilitators

Permitted Substances

Strict Limits:
  • • Only Psilocybe cubensis species
  • • No synthetic psilocybin
  • • No wild harvesting permitted
  • • Max 50mg psilocybin per session (~6g dried)

Prohibited in Oregon

  • • Only Psilocybe cubensis (other species banned)
  • • Synthetic psilocybin or lab-created
  • • Wild harvesting of mushrooms
  • • Take-home products (onsite only)
  • • Administration in incorporated cities (unincorporated only)

Oregon Specifics

  • • No medical referral required (wellness model)
  • • Out-of-state residents welcome
  • • Preparation session mandatory
  • • Integration session recommended
  • • Products: dried, capsules, tea, chocolates, extracts

Federal Legal Status

Psilocybin remains a Schedule I controlled substance under the federal Controlled Substances Act. State programs operate under state law only.

Banking Challenges Many centers operate cash-only due to federal banking restrictions.
Employment Risks Use may violate federal employment policies or security clearances.
Federal Property Illegal on federal land (national parks, federal buildings, airports).
